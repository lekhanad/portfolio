                Dodda Aladamara
             
 
          
 

    
    
Overview

    

        A magnificent oddity of nature, the Dodda Alada Mara in Bangalore is a 400-year-old banyan tree. Amazingly, the tree is spread across in more than 3 acres of land, making it one of the vastest Banyan trees ever. Found on the Bangalore Mysore road, the tree has become a great attraction for tourists.
        The structural integrity of the tree was hindered in the late 2000s when Dodda Alada Mara suffered through diseases and now the tree just looks like a bundle of a vast collection of trees. The enormous scatter of the tree has also made itself home to a number of animals including monkeys.
        Additionally,  there are seating arrangements placed around the tree which the visitors can use to sit and adore the beauty of Dodda Alada Mara and enjoy catching a glimpse of the monkeys. Picnics and evening walks are some of the most common activities appreciated here. Looking at the growing tourist visits, there are a number of restaurants and eateries arranged near and around the tree.
        Various plantations including a garden of roses are farmed around the tree. According to the Hindu tradition, the Banyan tree is holy and thus, apart from the general visitors, many devotees visit the big banyan tree.
        There is also a temple right in the epicenter of the tree where devotees can simply bask a peaceful time praying.
        All in all, the Dodda Alada Mara is a natural enigma standing tall for 4 centuries making it a must-visit for anyone who is visiting Bangalore or Mysore for holidays.
    

    
Haunted Stories of the Dodda Alada Mara:

    
Dodda Alada Mara being a 400-year-old establishment has its fair share of haunted and scary stories. Few says that the tourists who have taken photographs at and around the location have seen some kind of ghosts in the photos.
        There are also stories of spirits of individuals who were once killed in an accident on the highway near the Big Banyan Tree now, surround the tree and haunt the local residents of the place. There are also a lot of ancient haunted tales that encapsulate Dodda Alada Mara and are quite famous among the local residents.
        The residents have also dedicated a certain time late at night when witches come to roam around the 3 acres of land and haunt people who enter the premises. However, these are just stories and should not stop you from discovering the magnificence of the tree.
        There have been individuals who have tried their methods of manipulating photos and videos to prove the existence of the paranormal but nothing has ever surfaced. Although spooky, some might find the stories fascinating and visitors can also talk to the locals here to enjoy the latest tales and horrifying stories. 
    

    
Activities at dodda Aladamara

    
Encircled by more than 3 acres of land, the Dobba Alada Mara allows visitors to enjoy a number of activities 

        1. Picnic - The vast dry land covered by various branches of the tree provides for a perfect place to enjoy a calm and relaxing picnic amidst the huge limbs of the Dodda Alada Mara. Visitors from across the town treat the place as the perfect weekend getaway location with family and friends. 

        2. Sit and relax - Apart from fantastically maintained, the tree area is embedded with a number of benches and a clean seating arrangement. Visitors can simply unwind and admire the magnificence of Dodda Alada Mara. You can also enjoy the sunset and evening sceneries from around here. 

        3. Pray - There is a temple established right in the middle of the 3-acre Banyan tree land. Since the tree itself is holy for the Hindus, there are a number of devotees visiting the place quite frequently. The temple serves perfectly for them to enjoy a calm time praying. 

        4. Strolling around - The calming shed developed from the long branches of the tree makes it perfect for visitors to enjoy long walks at any time of the day. Especially in the evening when the soft wind blows through the trees and the sun is settling, walking down through Dodda Alada Mara during this time is majestical. 

        5. Monkey Spotting - The extended branches and lanky limps of Dodda Alada Mara have allowed monkeys to be a common affair here. Visitors can enjoy a fun time spotting them. The authorized also warn visitors to keep their food away from the monkeys since they are known for snapping it out of the tourist's hands. 

        6. Cycling - One of the more leisure yet adventurous activities to do around Dodda Alada Mara is cycling. There are several organizations that plan cycling activities here. The activity starts early in the morning and goes on for a couple of hours. Visitors can simply cycle through the enormous tree area to the Manchandela Dam allocated in the vicinity of Dodda Alada Mara.

        7. Enjoy Snacks and Meals - The place is visitors by a number of tourists and it is also established on the main Bangalore - Mysore Road. This has allowed a number of restaurants and snack shops to open around the area. It is perfect for visitors who have spent hours exploring the vast land to just relax, unwind and relish some delicious snacks and beverages while enjoying the ancestry of the tree.

        8. Photography - Since there is no restriction on taking pictures here, visitors can take several snaps to capture the beauty of the magnificent Dodda Alada Mara. 
    

    
Essential information

    

        Timings: 8:00 AM - 5:30 PM all day, every day. 

        Price: Free Entry 
 

        Tips For Visiting Dodda Alada Mara: 
 

        There is some necessary information which the visitors must know before journeying to Dodda Alada Mara. 

        Don't forget to wear loose clothes and shoes -The entire area is scattered with shreds of branches and small rocks. It is best if visitors wear shoes while walking around the place. Additionally, loose clothes are also a good option since the place can get really humid during the day. 

        Be aware of the monkeys - The area is surrounded by a bunch of monkeys and if you carry food around they might just jump in on you and snatch it. To be on the safer side be aware of them and try not to eat around the monkeys. 

        The best time to visit the place is between 9:00 AM and 12:00 PM in the morning and after 4:00 PM in the evening.
